Student Handbook

Protect Yourself, Help Your Friends

This is short, direct, and non-judgmental. Its aim is to give students practical options: keep safe now, help a friend, and access support without shame.

1. Quick Facts for Students

  • Experimentation is common, but not harmless. Some substances are dangerous even once.
  • If a friend is overdosing, call emergency services immediately and tell an adult.

2. How to Help a Friend

  • Ask directly if they are safe
  • Don't keep secrets about safety risks. Speak to a counselor
  • Offer to go with them to get help

3. If You're Worried About Yourself

"You are not weak. Asking for help is courage."
  • Start with a trusted teacher, school counselor, or the helpline on this site

4. Safety Actions

  • Avoid places where you feel pressured
  • Have exit plans for events
  • Keep friends who respect your choices

5. Peer Refusal Scripts (Short)

  • "No thanks — I've got practice tomorrow."
  • "I don't want that. Let's do something else."
  • "I can't — my family would freak."

6. Where to Go — Quick List

  • School counselor
  • Trusted elder or coach
  • Anonymous QR report (if you see a peddler)
  • Emergency services in overdose

Closing

You are worth protecting. Reach out. Help is confidential and there are people who want to help you get back on track.
