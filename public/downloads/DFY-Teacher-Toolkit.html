Teacher Toolkit

Prevent, Detect, Refer

Teachers are the frontline in identifying risk. This toolkit equips you with class-ready lesson plans, detection signals, and an immediate referral workflow that protects students and engages families.

1. Classroom Prevention Module (45–60 minutes)

Objective: Give students facts, refusal skills, and support options.

Structure:

  • 10-minute icebreaker
  • 20-minute facts + myth-busting
  • 10-minute role-play on refusal
  • 10-minute resource share

Materials:

  • Slide deck
  • Short video clip (2–3 minutes)
  • Printable one-pagers

2. Detecting Signs in School Settings

  • Frequent absences
  • Declining grades
  • Sudden aggression
  • Carrying unusual items
  • Visible drug paraphernalia in lockers

What to document:

  • Dates
  • Observed behavior
  • Impact on schoolwork
  • Witnesses

3. Immediate Steps for Teachers

  • Maintain student privacy
  • Inform the designated school counselor or welfare officer
  • Avoid confronting the student alone in public
  • Follow school safeguarding policy and escalate to principal if safety risk is present

4. Referral Flow (Simple, 6-Step)

  1. Observe & record
  2. Alert school counselor (confidential note)
  3. Counselor meets student privately
  4. Counselor contacts parents with school's safeguarding lead
  5. Joint plan: medical/behavior evaluation or family meeting
  6. Follow-up & documentation

Slide 1: "What is substance use?"

Short definitions and stigma warning

Slide 2: "How to support a friend"

Listening + connecting to trusted adults

Slide 3: "How to refuse"

Scripts and roleplay

Slide 4: "Where to get help"

Counselor, helpline, anonymous QR reporting

6. Managing Disclosures

  • Believe the student and thank them for trusting you
  • Keep details limited to safeguarding leads
  • Arrange immediate support and parental contact per policy

7. Teacher Self-Care & Boundaries

  • Don't take parental anger personally. Use protocols
  • Seek supervision or school counselor support — these cases are heavy work
